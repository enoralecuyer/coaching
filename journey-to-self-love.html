My Journey to Self-Love.


October 9, 2017

Today, I am a happy and healthy person, but it hasn't always been like that.

As a teenager, I struggled for years with depression, anxiety and eating disorder. Every day was consumed in thoughts over food, obsessive compulsive disorder or how bad a person I was. I was creating more and more lies to hide the truth of my daily life. I started losing friends and losing myself on the way.

Realizing that I had a choice: remaining or changing.

One day, I realized that nothing would change until I made a change in my life. I could have stayed the same person doing the same things over and over again, but I knew my life was more than those habits. I knew my life had infinite possibilities for change.

Taking responsibility for myself

First, I stopped calling myself a victim. Then, something fundamental changed: I owned my story. I started telling the truth to friends I trusted. I accepted the ups and downs of my life and my character. I became a more complex person in my own eyes and the eyes of others, but most importantly, I became whole with myself.

Starting to take better care of myself

I started reading books and doing research on what people do to feel happy, healthy and fulfilled. I applied those concepts into my life and, with a lot of trial and error, I began making drastic changes from the inside out.

Learning about myself.

I looked closely at my fears, doubts, and limiting patterns. I met new people, learned new ways of thinking and behaving, and transformed my actions and beliefs. I learned to love myself. My thoughts became more clear, sharp, and aligned with the person I wanted to be. Because I was so focused on growing, there was little space left for the old destructive thoughts. I reshaped my mind and the world was new under my feet.

This is what I changed in my life

  • Loving my body
  • Loving my feelings
  • Forgiving others
  • Forgiving myself
  • Speaking my truth
  • Giving myself permission
  • Taking care of myself first
  • Setting healthy boundaries
  • Reclaiming my time and space
  • Acting on my needs
  • Honoring my spirituality
  • Trusting myself
  • Taking ownership over my past
  • Creating the life I choose to live
  • Being responsible for my own feelings and no one else's.
  • Trusting my instinct and acting on it
  • Trying and trying again
  • Taking time to breathe and to rest
  • Taking time to play and have fun
  • Being kind and gentle to myself
  • Loving every part of me

    • Today

    Today I love who I am. My mind is clear and focused. I feel good about taking care of myself. I have control over my thoughts and my habits. I forgave myself and feel free and happy. I finally honor the fact that I am never responsible for someone else’s feelings but only for my own happiness. I create the life that I want. I love who I became.
